Overview

Waterade tracks a fleet of water-dispenser machines deployed across customer sites (mostly care homes). It is the single place to manage the whole life of every unit — from stock, to delivery as a trial or rental, through routine and reactive service, to replacement or write-off.

The building blocks

Customers
Homes & sites with a unique client reference.
Machines
Every unit, by serial, with full history.
Deliveries
Send a machine out as trial or rental.
Service Tickets
Collect, service and return machines.
Replacement Jobs
Swap a faulty unit while it's serviced.
Invoicing
Track rental invoices & plan by area.

Getting Around

A persistent menu moves you between the main areas. From anywhere you can reach:

  • Machines — the full fleet list (the app opens here).
  • Fleet Kanban — every machine grouped visually by status.
  • Machines Due for Service — units needing proactive service.
  • Service Tickets — all open and closed tickets (list & kanban).
  • Customers — every home / site.
  • Rentals — active contracts, with Invoice Tracking & Rentals Report.
  • Trials — active and historical trial deliveries.
  • Replacement Jobs — all replacement jobs across tickets.
  • Settings — master data (Customer Groups & Regions).
Most lists support search and filters, and every row has an action (an eye icon or button) to open its full detail view.

Customers

Customers are the locations that own, rent or trial dispensers. Each has a unique client reference (e.g. CH108, OAK107) that links machines, rentals, trials and tickets back to the home.

Create a customer

1
Open Customers → Add Customer.

Start a new customer record.

2
Fill in the details.

Home / site name, client reference, customer group, region, county and contacts (name, phone, email, address, postcode).

3
Save.

The client reference must be unique — a duplicate is blocked with a warning. Groups & regions come from Settings.

The customer profile

Opening a customer shows everything in one place: Customer Information (group, region, county, contacts, notes), Delivered Machines (every unit on site with status, delivery date and last service), Service History (all tickets with resolution), and an Add Rental shortcut.

A customer that still has machines or history linked cannot be deleted — the app blocks this with a Cannot Delete Customer message to protect the audit trail.

Machines

The Machines screen is the master fleet list. Each machine is identified by its serial number and also carries a friendly machine number, birth date, current status, current home, last service date, and component-replacement dates (fan, motor, condenser).

Add a machine

1
Machines → Add New Machine.
2
Enter serial number, machine number and birth date.

Set the initial status (typically new_stock).

3
Add Machine.

Serial and machine number must both be unique; missing required fields are flagged before saving.

Bulk import: use Import to upload many units from a spreadsheet at once. Filter & search: narrow by status or region, or search by serial / machine number, then click a row to open its detail view.

Machine Detail & History

The detail view is the complete record for one unit:

  • Machine Identity — serial, machine age, birth date, last serviced, current status.
  • Component Replacements — when the fan, motor and condenser were last replaced (or "Never").
  • Current Location — the home, when it was sent, and days on site.
  • Comments & Notes — free-text notes against the machine.

Correcting the serial or machine number

Click Edit → Edit Machine Identity to correct a serial or machine number — only available while the machine is New Stock, and the new values must stay unique. Correct Serial fixes a mis-recorded serial on a machine already in the field.

Act on a machine

Jump straight into View Full History (the complete service timeline), Create Service Ticket, Allocate, or View Customer Profile for the site it is deployed at.

Fleet Kanban

A visual board of every machine grouped by status. Each card shows the serial, the home it's at, and how long it has been in the current state. Use it to see where the fleet is bottlenecked — a tall With Us to Be Serviced column means intake is backed up. Click a card to open the machine, or switch to List View any time.

The kanban is a read-only overview — status changes happen through tickets, rentals, trials and deliveries, not by dragging cards.

Deliveries

The fast path for getting a machine out to a customer as a trial or a rental. The list shows every delivery with date sent, home, serial, type, status and (for trials) the end date, and highlights Trials Ending Soon.

1
New Delivery.
2
Choose the Delivery Type — Trial or Rental.
3
Select the machine and customer.
4
Set the dates.

For a trial the end date is set automatically to 28 days from the delivery date. For a rental, fill in the rental details.

5
Create Delivery.

The machine flips to the right deployed status and appears in the Trials or Rentals list.

From the list you can also Convert a trial to a rental or Return a machine.

Trials

Short-term loaner deployments — a fixed one-month (28-day) duration — for prospects evaluating a machine. The list shows customer, client ref, serial, machine number, delivery date, tracking number, end date and status, and calls out Trials Ending Soon with days left.

1
New Trial (or a Trial delivery) sends the machine out; the end date is set 28 days ahead.
2
At the end, Convert to a rental (the machine stays deployed) — or recall it.
3
Schedule Collection.

Enter the planned collection date and courier tracking number.

4
Received → Confirm Received.

Mark the unit received at the workshop and record the return reason.

Rentals

Machines deployed on a paid, ongoing basis. The list shows customer, client ref, serial & machine number, shipping / delivery dates, tracking number, rental cost, first invoice date, invoice status, status, and (once ended) returned date and reason.

Create a rental

1
New Rental (or Add Rental from a customer's profile).
2
Select customer & machine, set dates, tracking and rental cost.
3
Create Rental.

The machine becomes deployed and appears in the active list.

Manage a live rental

  • Edit Invoice Details — update first invoice date, invoice number and completion status.
  • Replace Machine (wrong serial) → Confirm Swap — correct a rental recorded against the wrong unit.
  • Schedule Collection from Customer — when a rental ends, enter the collection date and tracking.
  • Mark Received at Workshop → Confirm Received — close out the return once the machine is back.

Invoicing & Reports

Two reports, reached from the Rentals screen, support billing:

Invoice Tracking

Lists each rented machine with its client reference (CRN), region, delivered date, first invoice date, invoice frequency, rental cost, previous invoice date, the next invoice date and how long until it is due — so you can see which rentals are coming up for invoicing.

Rentals Report

Lists all customers with active rentals, grouped for invoicing — CRN, region, customer group, customer name and total rental cost. Drill into a customer to see their rental detail, then jump to the full profile.

Creating a Service Ticket

A ticket is opened whenever machines at a customer need attention. Creation is a guided four-step form:

1

Customer & Machine Selection

Pick the customer, review their details (site, account ref, group, region, machine summary), then tick every machine that needs attention.

2

Call & Request Details

Record when the call was received, what was requested (WA Requested), the customer reference number and the quantity to be serviced.

3

Reported Faults

Select all the faults the customer reported.

4

Issue Details

Describe the issue as reported and record any advice given to the customer.

Click Add Service Ticket to submit. Required fields are validated first. On submit the ticket is created with status In transit to us and a Job Sheet is generated automatically.

You can also start a ticket from a machine's detail view, or from the Service Tickets Kanban with New Ticket.

Service Ticket Workflow

The ticket detail view is where a ticket is worked from creation to closure. The header shows a live tally of the machines across the stages: Total, In Transit, Awaiting Intake, Ready for Service, In Service, Completed, Written Off. The end-to-end sequence:

1

Schedule Collection

Set the date the faulty machine is to be collected from the customer site.

2

Receive Machines

When units arrive, record the date received. The ticket moves to With Us to Be Serviced and each machine updates.

3

Verify Intake

For each machine, confirm the physical unit matches the record and add optional intake notes. It becomes Ready for Service.

4

Generate Service Report

Set service type, owner, category, location, technician and dates. Produces a Draft Service Report and marks the machine In Service.

5

Complete Service or Write Off

Close out each machine. When every machine reaches one of these, the ticket auto-closes.

Other actions on a ticket

  • Close as Phone Support — resolved on the phone: record the advice and close without collecting.
  • Relabel Serial (New Panel Fitted) — assign a new serial & machine number when a panel is replaced.
  • Correct Serial — fix a mis-recorded serial on a received machine.
  • Replacement — jump to Replacement Jobs to swap a machine out.
  • Report / View Draft / View Final — open or download the job sheet and service reports.

An audit entry is recorded at each transition, so you can always trace who did what and when.

Service Reports (Draft & Final)

Each serviced machine produces two PDF reports with different purposes:

ReportGenerated whenPurpose
Draft Service ReportOffice clicks Generate Service Report on a verified machine.Printable working sheet carried with the machine. Includes ticket number, customer, serial & machine number, service type, technician and dates — the engineer marks it up by hand with findings, parts replaced and notes.
Final Service ReportOffice clicks Complete Service after the marked-up draft comes back.Permanent digital record. Consolidates the transcribed technician notes, parts replaced with dates, completion date, remarks and any service photos into one archived PDF.

The Complete Service popup captures the technician notes, fan / motor / condenser parts replaced with dates, completion date, up to ten service photos, and completion remarks. Both reports open from the machine's card via View Draft Service Report and View Final Service Report.

End to end: machine arrives → intake verified → print Draft Report → engineer services & writes notes on it → office transcribes notes, ticks parts replaced, uploads photos in Complete Service → Final Report PDF is generated and stored against the machine.
On closure the repaired machine returns to in_stock and is held in the warehouse — it is not auto-reissued to the original customer. Any replacement sent earlier stays deployed on site.

Replacement Jobs

When a machine must be swapped rather than repaired on site, you create a Replacement Job. A job ties one or more replacement machines to a parent ticket and tracks courier & tracking details for each swap.

1
From the ticket, open Replacement.

Reaches the replacement-jobs screen for that ticket.

2
Assign a replacement for each machine.

Pick an in-stock unit and enter shipping details (courier, tracking) and send date. The card turns blue once saved.

3
Create Replacement Job.

Available once all machines are assigned. Use Send Replacement to dispatch.

4
Close Job.

Completes the job when finished.

All replacement jobs across every ticket are listed under Replacement Jobs in the menu, each opening a detail view with its assignments and shipping status.

Due for Service & Planning by Area

Machines Due for Service lists units needing proactive service based on their last service date — serial, home, location, client ref, last serviced and how far due. This drives scheduled maintenance rather than waiting for a fault.

From there, Plan by Area opens Service Planning by Area, which groups due machines by home and postcode with a count of machines due — so a visit can cover several nearby sites in one trip.

Settings (Master Data)

The Settings screen manages the curated lists used across the customer form — keeping them tidy keeps customer data consistent.

  • Customer Groups — add, rename, delete and import groups. Shows how many customers use each; a group still in use can't be deleted; duplicate names are blocked.
  • Regions — add, rename, delete and import regions with their codes. Duplicate names or codes are blocked with a conflict warning.
