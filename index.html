Overview

Waterade tracks a fleet of water-dispenser machines deployed across customer sites (mostly care homes). The app is the single place to manage the whole lifecycle of every unit — from stock, through delivery to a customer as a trial or rental, through routine and reactive service, to replacement or write-off.

The core building blocks are:

  • Customers — the homes / locations where machines are deployed, each with a unique client reference.
  • Machines — every individual unit, identified by its serial number, with full history and component-replacement tracking.
  • Deliveries, Trials & Rentals — how a machine gets to a customer and on what commercial basis.
  • Service Tickets — the workflow for collecting, receiving, servicing, and returning machines.
  • Replacement Jobs — swapping a faulty unit for a working one while the original is serviced.
  • Invoicing & Planning — rental invoice tracking and proactive service planning by area.
The typical lifecycle: a machine is added to stock → delivered to a customer as a trial or rental → serviced periodically or when a fault is reported (via a service ticket) → optionally swapped out with a replacement job → eventually collected back to stock or written off.

Getting Around

The app uses a persistent menu to move between the main areas. From anywhere you can reach:

  • Machines — the full fleet list (the app opens here by default).
  • Fleet Kanban — every machine grouped visually by status.
  • Machines Due for Service — units needing proactive service.
  • Service Tickets — all open and closed tickets (list and kanban).
  • Customers — every home / site.
  • Rentals — active rental contracts (with Invoice Tracking and Rentals Report).
  • Trials — active and historical trial deliveries.
  • Replacement Jobs — all replacement jobs across tickets.
  • Settings — master data (Customer Groups & Regions).

Most list screens support searching and filtering, and every row has an action (usually an eye icon or a button) to open the full detail view for that record.

Customers

Customers represent the locations that own, rent or trial dispensers. Each customer has a unique client reference (e.g. CH108, OAK107) used across the system to link machines, rentals, trials and tickets back to a home.

Creating a new customer

  1. Open Customers and click Add Customer.
  2. Fill in the home / site name, client reference, customer group, region, county and contact details (contact name, phone, email, address, postcode).
  3. Add any notes, then Add Customer. The client reference must be unique — a duplicate is blocked with a warning.

The customer groups and regions available in the dropdowns are managed centrally under Settings.

The customer detail view

Opening a customer shows their full profile in one place:

  • Customer Information — group, region, county, contact, phone, email, address, postcode and notes.
  • Delivered Machines — every unit currently at that site with serial, machine number, status, delivery date and last service.
  • Service History — all tickets raised for that customer with date of call, machines involved and resolution.
  • Add Rental — start a new rental for the customer directly from their profile.
A customer that still has machines or history linked to it cannot be deleted — the app blocks this with a Cannot Delete Customer message to protect the audit trail.

Machines

The Machines screen is the master fleet list. Each machine is uniquely identified by its serial number and also carries a friendly machine number, birth date, current status, the home it is currently at, last service date and component-replacement dates (fan, motor, condenser).

Creating a new machine

  1. On the Machines screen click Add New Machine.
  2. Enter the serial number, machine number and birth date, and set the initial status (typically new_stock).
  3. Click Add Machine. Both the serial number and the machine number must be unique — duplicates are blocked, and any missing required fields are flagged before saving.

Importing machines in bulk

Use the Import button on the Machines screen to upload many units at once from a spreadsheet, instead of adding them one by one.

Filtering and searching

Use the status and region filters at the top of the list to narrow the fleet, or search by serial / machine number. Click a row to open the machine's full detail view.

Machine Detail & History

The machine detail view is the complete record for a single unit:

  • Machine Identity — serial number, machine age, birth date, last serviced date and current status.
  • Component Replacements — when the fan, motor and condenser were last replaced (or "Never").
  • Current Location — the home it was delivered to, when it was sent, and how many days it has been on site.
  • Comments & Notes — free-text notes recorded against the machine.

Correcting the serial or machine number

Click Edit → Edit Machine Identity to correct a serial or machine number. This is only available while the machine is New Stock, and the new values must still be unique. There is also a Correct Serial option for fixing a mis-recorded serial on a machine already in the field.

Acting on a machine

From the detail view you can jump straight into common actions: View Full History (the complete service timeline), Create Service Ticket for the machine, Allocate it, or View Customer Profile for the site it is currently deployed at.

Fleet Kanban

The Fleet Kanban gives a visual board of every machine grouped by its current status. Each card shows the serial number, the home it is at, and how long it has been in the current state. Use it for an at-a-glance picture of where the fleet is bottlenecked — for example, a tall With Us to Be Serviced column means intake is backed up. Click any card to open the machine's detail view, or switch to List View at any time.

The kanban is a read-only overview — status changes still happen through service tickets, rentals, trials and deliveries, not by dragging cards.

Deliveries

The Deliveries screen is the fast path for getting a machine out to a customer, whether as a trial or a rental. It lists all deliveries with the date sent, home, serial, type, status and (for trials) the trial end date, and highlights Trials Ending Soon so nothing slips.

Creating a delivery

  1. Click New Delivery.
  2. Choose the Delivery Type — Trial or Rental.
  3. Select the machine and the customer.
  4. Set the dates. For a trial, the trial end date is automatically set to 28 days from the delivery date. For a rental, fill in the rental details.
  5. Click Create Delivery. The machine flips to the appropriate deployed status and appears in the Trials or Rentals lists.

From the Deliveries list you can also Convert a trial to a rental or Return a machine.

Trials

Trials are short-term loaner deployments — a fixed one-month (28-day) duration — used when a prospective customer wants to evaluate a machine before committing. The Trials screen lists every trial with customer, client reference, serial and machine number, delivery date, tracking number, trial end date and status, and calls out Trials Ending Soon with the days left.

Running a trial end-to-end

  1. New Trial (or a Trial delivery) sends the machine out; the trial end date is set 28 days ahead.
  2. When the trial concludes you either Convert it to a rental (the machine stays deployed) or recall the machine.
  3. To recall, click Schedule Collection — enter the planned collection date and the courier tracking number.
  4. When the unit arrives back, click Received → Confirm Received to mark it received at the workshop and record the return reason.

Rentals

Rentals cover machines deployed on a paid, ongoing basis. The Rentals screen lists every active rental contract with customer, client reference, serial and machine number, shipping and delivery dates, tracking number, rental cost, first invoice date, invoice status, status, and (once ended) returned date and reason.

Creating a rental

  1. Click New Rental (or Add Rental from a customer's profile).
  2. Select the customer and the machine, and set the shipping / delivery dates, tracking number and rental cost.
  3. Click Create Rental. The machine status becomes deployed and the rental appears in the active list.

Managing a live rental

  • Edit Invoice Details — update the first invoice date, invoice number and completion status for billing.
  • Replace Machine (wrong serial) → Confirm Swap — correct the rental if the wrong unit was recorded against it.
  • Schedule Collection from Customer — when a rental ends, enter the collection date and tracking number.
  • Mark Received at Workshop → Confirm Received — close out the return once the machine is back.

Invoice Tracking & Rentals Report

Two reports, reachable from the Rentals screen, support billing:

Invoice Tracking

Invoice Tracking lists each rented machine with its client reference (CRN), region, delivered date, first invoice date, invoice frequency, rental cost, the previous invoice date, the next invoice date and how long until it is due. Use it to see at a glance which rentals are coming up for invoicing.

Rentals Report

The Rentals Report lists all customers with active rentals, grouped for invoicing — showing CRN, region, customer group, customer name and total rental cost. Drill into a customer to see their rental detail (Customer Rentals view), and from there jump to the full customer profile.

Creating a Service Ticket

A service ticket is opened whenever one or more machines at a customer need attention. Creation is a guided four-step form:

  1. Step 1 — Customer & Machine Selection. Pick the customer, review their details (site name, account ref, group, region, and a summary of their machines — total, deployed, trials, under service), then tick every machine that needs attention on this visit.
  2. Step 2 — Call & Request Details. Record when the call was received, what was requested (WA Requested), the customer reference number and the quantity to be serviced.
  3. Step 3 — Reported Faults. Select all the faults the customer reported.
  4. Step 4 — Issue Details. Describe the issue as reported, and record any advice given to the customer.

Click Add Service Ticket to submit. Required fields are validated first (a Required details missing prompt appears if anything essential is blank). On submit the ticket is created with status In transit to us and a Job Sheet is generated automatically.

You can also start a ticket straight from a machine's detail view, or from the Service Tickets Kanban using New Ticket.

Service Ticket Workflow

The ticket detail view is where a ticket is worked from creation to closure. The header shows the ticket number, customer, priority, dates and the reported issue, and a live tally of the machines on the ticket across the stages: Total, In Transit, Awaiting Intake, Ready for Service, In Service, Completed, Written Off.

The end-to-end sequence is:

  1. Schedule Collection — set or update the date the faulty machine is to be collected from the customer site.
  2. Receive Machines — when the units arrive at the workshop, record the date received. The ticket moves to With Us to Be Serviced and each machine is updated.
  3. Verify Intake — for each received machine, confirm the physical unit matches the record and add optional intake notes. The machine becomes Ready for Service.
  4. Generate Service Report — set the service type, owner, category, location, technician and dates. This produces a Draft Service Report and marks the machine In Service.
  5. Complete Service or Write Off — close out each machine. When every machine reaches one of these, the ticket auto-closes.

Other actions on a ticket

  • Close as Phone Support — if the issue is resolved over the phone, record the advice given and close the ticket without collecting the machine.
  • Relabel Serial (New Panel Fitted) — assign a new serial and machine number when a machine's panel is replaced.
  • Correct Serial — fix a mis-recorded serial on a received machine.
  • Replacement — jump to Replacement Jobs to swap a machine out (see below).
  • Report / View Draft Service Report / View Final Service Report — open or download the ticket's job sheet and the service reports.

An audit entry is recorded at each transition, so you can always trace who did what and when from the ticket.

Service Reports (Draft & Final)

Each serviced machine produces two distinct PDF reports that serve different purposes in the workflow:

ReportGenerated whenPurpose
Draft Service Report Office clicks Generate Service Report on a verified machine. Printable working sheet carried with the machine for the engineer. Includes the ticket number, customer, machine serial & number, service type, technician and dates — the engineer marks it up by hand with findings, parts replaced and notes.
Final Service Report Office clicks Complete Service after the engineer returns the marked-up draft. Permanent digital record of the completed service. Consolidates the technician notes (transcribed by the office from the engineer's paper), parts replaced with dates, completion date, completion remarks and any uploaded service photos into a single archived PDF.

The Complete Service popup captures the technician notes, the fan / motor / condenser parts replaced with dates, the service completion date, up to ten service photos, and completion remarks. Both reports are accessible from the machine's card via View Draft Service Report and View Final Service Report.

End-to-end flow: machine arrives → intake verified → office generates & prints the Draft Report → engineer services the machine and writes notes on the printout → office receives the marked-up paper → office opens Complete Service, transcribes the notes, ticks off parts replaced, uploads photos → the Final Report PDF is generated and stored against the machine.
On ticket closure the repaired machine returns to in_stock and is held in the warehouse — it is not automatically reissued to the original customer. Any replacement that was sent out earlier stays deployed at the customer site.

Replacement Jobs

When a machine has to be swapped out rather than repaired on site, you create a Replacement Job. A job ties one or more replacement machines to a parent service ticket and tracks the courier and tracking details for each swap.

How replacement jobs work

  1. From the service ticket, open Replacement to reach the replacement-jobs screen for that ticket.
  2. Click Assign on each machine to pick its replacement from in-stock units and enter the shipping details (courier and tracking) and send date. The card turns blue once the replacement is saved.
  3. Once all machines on the ticket are assigned, click Create Replacement Job to finalise it. Use Send Replacement to dispatch.
  4. When the job is finished, Close Job completes it.

All replacement jobs across every ticket are listed under Replacement Jobs in the menu, each opening a detail view with its machine assignments and shipping status.

Due for Service & Planning by Area

Machines Due for Service lists units that need proactive service based on their last service date, showing serial, home name, location, client reference, last serviced date and how far due they are. This drives scheduled maintenance rather than waiting for a fault to be reported.

From there, Plan by Area opens Service Planning by Area, which groups the due machines by home and postcode with a count of machines due and the last serviced date — so a visit can be planned to cover several nearby sites in one trip.

Settings (Master Data)

The Settings screen manages the curated lists used across the customer form. Keeping these tidy keeps customer data consistent.

  • Customer Groups — add, rename, delete and import groups. The list shows how many customers use each group; a group still in use cannot be deleted. Adding a duplicate name is blocked with a warning.
  • Regions — add, rename, delete and import regions with their region codes. Duplicate region names or codes are blocked with a conflict warning.
